Finish Well

(Hebrews 12:1) Wherefore seeing we also are compassed about with so great a cloud of witnesses, let us lay aside every weight, and the sin which doth so easily beset us, and let us run with patience the race that is set before us,

An athlete might train with weights in preparation for the final match, but no runner, no matter how good they are, will run the final race with ankle and wrist weights on. These things slow the runner down, they cause increased drag and resistance, and when a runner has been bound by extra weight, the moment he takes them off he feels the difference and runs freely. Jesus Christ is looking on, the saints that have gone on before are looking on, the angels are looking on, and when no one else is around you are looking on yourself! At the last day the entire creation will look on, our works will be revealed and tried by fire and the race we have so patiently run will be more than worthwhile.

Do not grow weary, worn warrior...

(Ephesians 6:12) For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places.

This olympic event is not a battle lost and won in the realm of the flesh. Many in the struggle start to strive against the wrong things and for the wrong things. Do not grow weary in well doing but conquer evil with good. Like Jacob wrestled with the angel for the blessing, wrestle with the things of God for spiritual blessings, increase, and favor. That is the kind of limp that changes and transforms for the better. When God touches the flesh, He takes away some of our natural strength and replaces it with His supernatural strength. It's so easy to get like Rachel and Leah, it's so easy to get so overly focused on the natural, and what is happening in the natural, and what we want to happen in the natural, and what is not happening in the natural, that we totally miss the supernatual and the true blessings that God has for us.

11 I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep. 12 But he that is an hireling, and not the shepherd, whose own the sheep are not, seeth the wolf coming, and leaveth the sheep, and fleeth: and the wolf catcheth them, and scattereth the sheep. 13 The hireling fleeth, because he is an hireling, and careth not for the sheep. 14 I am the good shepherd, and know my sheep, and am known of mine. 15 As the Father knoweth me, even so know I the Father: and I lay down my life for the sheep. 16 And other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring, and they shall hear my voice; and there shall be one fold, and one shepherd. (John 10:11-16) One scene, two perspectives. One perspective is the unseen, supernatural realm, we might not always see or know when the enemy is in operation but we can see and feel the effects of it. Imagine the hot breath of the invisible predator, ready to devour it's unsuspecting foe. But not far behind, closer than we even think, the good Shepard is fighting on our behalf, on His way to the rescue. Like David in the field, He takes out the lions, the bears, and the giants in our lives when we let Him. The other perspective is the natural realm. The sheep had no say in their rescue but the brothers of David did and Eliab, David's older brother tried with all of His might to derail the plan and purpose of God. If he had succeeded, what would have transpired that day? That was the unseen wolf breathing down the neck of the sheep but the Master was close by and on the way with a mighty deliverance.

Many men have started well but not completed the race the same way they began and it was almost all for nought. There is not a man alive save the Lord Jesus Chrsit who has not made some kind of mistake, and so it is not about that but it is about the heart.The key difference between a man after God's own heart that slips and falls and someone who is not after God's heart is attitude and posture. Only a Mary can sustain the work that a Martha is trying to do and Jesus says that sitting at His feet is the better part that will not be taken away from those that pursue Him. Supernatural wisdom without the word of God to lead and guide is not sustainable and open rebellion and disobedience to the word of God is disastrous. The wisdom of Solomon was totally corrupted in the end because he was too smart to obey the word and heed the warnings given to him. David was in a tight spot but the worldly wisdom of Ahithophel was overturned because David trusted in God to do it through a chosen vessel. David was a man after God's own heart despite his faults, failures, and weaknesses. He started out with the sheep and the word of God. He held tight to the Lord and His word when on the run from Saul. The first half of David's testimony contains great examples of how to walk, think and waht to do. The second half of David's testimony contains lots of examples of things not to do, not for his condemnation but for our edification. Many kings fell and went astray, and they all got off course because they veered and steered away from God's word, will, and way.
